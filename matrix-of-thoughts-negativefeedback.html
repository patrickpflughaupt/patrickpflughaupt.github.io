Escaping the Negative Feedback Loop



My subconscious used to be part of the Matrix, it still is to some extent. A genuine need to be wired into the virtual reality, one where I can feel like I have close to a thousand “friends”, a like on a filtered and perfectly angled picture of myself to fill the void. A like that soon wasn’t even enough; I needed explicit “reactions” to my picture like a heart to fill an even bigger void. Constantly longing for acceptance, entitlement and nirvana. 

More than a decade after joining Facebook and its minions, I realised that the very desire for a more positive experience is itself a negative experience. Paradoxically, the acceptance of one’s negative experience is itself a positive experience. This uncomfortable yet provoking thought from The Subtle Art of Not Giving a Fuck resonated with me harmoniously. The book aims the reader to become more conscious about their thoughts, decisions and actions; a book to help you understand that life is an endless series of problems, some better than others, and you try to solve them by the art of living your life. It’s about perspective. If you want to change how you perceive your problems, you have to change what you truly value and how you measure failure and success. 

To visualise this, watch Netflix’s The Social Dilemma. A documentary powerful enough to earn its own genre, one that shares its podium with non-fiction horror. To project a life of myself decades from now where I’m stuck in a Negative Feedback Loop inside the Matrix - I found it distressing and gruesome.

It has helped me remove social media from my phone, read more books, exercise my curiosity and become more self-aware.

Don’t just sit there. Do something. The answers will follow.
