Tenet: Feel It



Don’t try to understand it. Feel it.

Covid-19 challenged the status quo in our society much like how Nolan challenges the status quo of cinema standards. Nolan brings theoretical physics accessible to the general audience and applies it to our world through Tenet. The scientific concept is surprisingly straightforward to follow. It’s based on the 2nd law of thermodynamics which states that the total entropy of an isolated system can never decrease over time, and is constant if and only if all processes are reversible; entropy is a function of time. In Tenet, Nolan explores the reverse of this concept - what would a world look like if we inverse local entropy through time while the total entropy still increases over time? 

What makes this movie complicated lies within the intricacies of this physical concept. If the present self moves forward while another version of you moves inversely in time to you into the past and inverts again to move forward while still stuck in the past, we have 3 simultaneous versions of you. This is the point at which things get interesting - a birth of questions that you never would think about otherwise. How do you breathe inverted oxygen? What happens if you meet your past self? How far back can you go? This is where Nolan’s creativity gets put into practice and keeps the audience alive with our unlimited number of theories; an Inception of a Matrix.

I admit, I found it complicated to follow the timeline of each character in chronology and thus, understand the movie. Watching the movie for the first time with laser focus to understand the full-loop timeline of the movie is difficult and in my opinion, not its purpose; Tenet is not a movie, it’s an experience of time. 

You need to feel it.
