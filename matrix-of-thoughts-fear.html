Fear: Perspectives of War Battles



I will never quit. Fear is a force that sharpens your senses. Being afraid is a state of paralysis in which you can't do anything.

Marcus Luttrell crawled at snail-speed into the nearest cover along the Sawtalo Sar Mountains, knowing he was the lone survivor and hunted by the hundreds of Taliban fighters within his radius. Blood still poured out of his legs like a fountain and bones were broken along his back and face; his only survival tool was not his rifle in his hand, it was his mind. The very same mind that pulled him through Navy SEALs’ Hell Week, the one that trained him to get back up every time he gets knocked over. 

I just cannot begin to fathom the state of fear his mind was engraved in when he was out there all on his own. But it was this fear-fuelled mind that navigated him forwards in the search for help, rescue and even the most basic to human survival: water. He was so dehydrated that his tongue was stuck to the roof of his mouth. If his mind didn’t propel him forwards, he would have eventually been found and killed by the Taliban; likely not a slow one either. Ultimately, there was no choice other than not quitting, drawing on every remaining ounce of strength and accomplishing the mission.

Although from a different perspective of a war battle, I believe Dr. David Nott, in War Doctor, was guided by the same force as Marcus. On his very first humanitarian mission, he was deployed to Sarajevo in the middle of the Bosnian War. On one day, he recounts a young boy being rushed into the hospital which he had to take care of. The kid had metal fragments in his abdomen from one of the mortars and had to be operated on immediately. As Dr. Nott began the incision, an explosion went off at the hospital. The room turned pitch black. Moments later he realised his nurses and assistants on the operation fled the operating theatre for cover while he was alone next to the kid, which now had died. Dr. David was as vulnerable as any civilian trying to survive the vicious conditions in his surroundings but he had the state of mind unlike anyone else. A force strong enough to navigate you through life-and-death decisions.

Fear to guide your strengths.
